About Me

I'm a high school student passionate about building software, designing games, and solving problems creatively. I enjoy blending technology with creativity to build projects that inspire, challenge, and make an impact.

Creator

I love turning imagination into something people can actually see, play, and experience. Whether I'm designing a game world, painting a digital scene, or sketching interface ideas late at night, creating is how I make sense of the world around me. Every project starts as a rough idea—a sketch, a mechanic, a mood—and slowly takes shape through iteration, feedback, and a lot of trial and error. I'm drawn to the moments where art and technology overlap, where a good design decision can make software feel alive. Building things that spark curiosity or bring a little joy to someone else's day is what keeps me coming back to the canvas, the code editor, and everything in between.

Thinker

I enjoy breaking down complex problems, exploring different perspectives, and finding practical solutions through logic and creativity. Whether I'm learning a new programming concept, designing a game mechanic, or brainstorming ideas for my next project, curiosity drives everything I do. I believe every challenge is an opportunity to learn, grow, and discover a better way of doing things. By asking questions, experimenting with different approaches, and thinking critically, I continue to develop solutions that are both innovative and effective.

Collaborator

I believe ideas have the greatest impact when they're transformed into something real. I enjoy turning concepts into working applications, writing clean code, experimenting with new technologies, and continuously improving my projects through testing, feedback, and iteration. Every build is an opportunity to strengthen my technical skills, solve real-world problems, and create experiences that are both functional and meaningful. I believe that learning by building is one of the best ways to grow as a developer and innovator.
